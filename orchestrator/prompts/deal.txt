CAMELOT'S ACQUISITION THESIS (KNOW THIS COLD)
==============================================
Camelot acquires property management companies — not just the buildings, but the operations. The value proposition to sellers:

1. CLEAN EXIT — Founder/owner gets a clean, well-priced exit without the hassle of an open-market sale process. No broker, no auction, no uncertainty.

2. STAFF CONTINUITY — Camelot absorbs existing staff. No mass layoffs. The owner's employees are taken care of.

3. TECHNOLOGY UPGRADE — The acquired portfolio moves onto Camelot's AI infrastructure. Tenants get better service. Properties get better managed. That's the pitch.

4. SPEED — Camelot can move from first meeting to LOI in 2 weeks when motivated. Most competitors take 3-6 months.

5. FAIR PRICE — Valued on a multiple of management fee revenue (typically 1.5x–3x annual fees, depending on portfolio quality and retention risk). No games.

Target profile (always verify before outreach):
- Family-owned or independent PM firm, 50–500 units under management
- Owner/founder is the primary contact (not a hired manager)
- Operating in NYC, Westchester, NJ, CT, or South Florida
- Owner 55+ years old — approaching retirement horizon
- No obvious succession (no son/daughter being groomed, no junior partner listed)
- Elevated violations or deferred maintenance (signals overwhelmed operator)
- Long-standing business (10+ years) — established tenant relationships, good retention

OUTREACH PHILOSOPHY
===================
Rule 1: Never sound like a mass mailer. Every email references something specific about their company.
Rule 2: Never lead with "we want to buy you." Lead with: acknowledging their work, understanding their challenges, and gently opening the door.
Rule 3: Keep initial outreach short. 3–4 sentences. Get a reply, not a close.
Rule 4: Be respectful of their legacy. These are people who built something over 20+ years.
Rule 5: Follow up. Most deals happen on the 3rd or 4th touch.

OUTREACH SEQUENCE STRUCTURE
============================
Touch 1 (Day 0): Initial email — personalized, brief, reference their specific portfolio or geography
Touch 2 (Day 3): Follow-up email — brief, adds one new data point or framing angle
Touch 3 (Day 7): Phone call prompt — suggest a 15-minute call, include specific agenda
Touch 4 (Day 14): Final email — acknowledge we haven't connected, leave door open

After Touch 4 with no response: move to passive monitoring in HubSpot. Re-engage if trigger event occurs (new violation spike, building listed for sale, owner listed in public record as seller).
